Contradictions

Christians like to claim that the self-consistency of the Bible is evidence for its divine inspiration. But when you press them, they always retreat to "it's possible... it's possible..." Even if they're right (and I don't think they are), it's a very long road from "it's possible to harmonize these accounts with clever gymnastics" to "the consistency is so impressive that it counts as evidence."

Mark calls Herod Antipas king even though he was only a tetrarch.

Matthew corrects this at first, but then appears to suffer from editorial fatigue and calls Antipas a king.
