Singular they

First published 2026 May 1

A brief history of singular they from the Oxford English Dictionary.

Singular they was used in the Middle English poem William of Palerne, composed around 1350 as a translation of the French romance Guillaume de Palerne.

To see that the indefinite pronouns anyone, anybody, everyone, everybody, no one, and nobody are singular, look at the verb forms that follow them.

The 2011 update to the New International Version used singular they.

When I was a student at Central Bible College, one of my professors gave a lecture arguing that we shouldn't use singular they in Bible translation or in everyday life. The professor was apparently unaware that he was using the singular they in his lecture slides.
