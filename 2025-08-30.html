One of my ethical principles is this: if an attribute or identity of a person is not something that one should make fun of, then there is no special pass for this when the person is bad. For instance, I think that it is wrong to shame people for their bodies, not only when we consider them good people, but also when we consider them bad people. When one does this, one is saying to other people with that body type, "Your body type is worthy of laughter, but because you're nice, we've generously agreed not to make fun of you." Someone might object that these jokes are chosen because they hit the people in power where it hurts. In other words, it's justified as a regrettable tactic to use against tyrants. There are several problems with this objection. The first is that body shaming and similar acts will primarily be heard, not by the tyrants, but by others in our community. The second is that there are much better things to make fun of tyrants for, such as their hypocrisy, their lies, their selfishness, their short-sightedness, and their carelessness. Targeting these behaviors highlights what is actually objectionable about the system. The third problem is that this tactic provides fodder to our enemies. They can say, "Look, the left are hypocrites, they're only pretending to be inclusive. What they call bigotry is just human nature."

Persuasion

Persuasion is... to voluntarily change their behavior without coercion.

Rational persuasion occurs when we attempt to persuade others by giving them good reasons for changing their behavior.

Honest persuasion occurs when we believe the statements we are making, and when our strategy of persuasion is such that others would not feel tricked if they were aware of it. This latter criterion can be helped by explicitly telling the audience what our strategy of persuasion is. I think that using honest persuasion requires that one use rational persuasion.

What does it mean to give someone a good reason for changing their behavior? I adhere to the Humean concept of action, which is that a person has a good reason to take an action when they believe that the action is an effective means for obtaining something that they desire. So there are two components here, instrumental beliefs and desires.

When should we use honest persuasion? Honest persuasion becomes less possible when our values or desires diverge too wildly from the audience. At the extreme, for example, fascists do not disagree with others about the best way to create a free and equal society. They do not want a free and equal society in the first place. It might still be possible to persuade someone to abandon fascism if we share other values, but the less values we share, the harder that gets.
