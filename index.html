Ten years ago, during my spring semester at Asbury Theological Seminary in Wilmore, Kentucky, I stopped believing in God.

ethics and morality

is there a difference between ethics and morality?

there's a discussion of this in the entry on modern morality and ancient ethics in the internet encyclopedia of philosophy.

the greek ethikos was the adjectival form of ethos, "character".

cicero translated this as moralis, the adjectival form of latin mores, "habits and customs."

the greek word was later borrowed into latin more directly as ethica, and then both terms came into english through old french.

according to the entry, different philosophers have distinguished between these terms in various ways.

i suspect that the important difference in usage is that ethics is used more in academic contexts while morality is more common and is found in both academic and popular contexts.

because of that, it seems like ethics is more associated with theory while morality is more associated with the subject of ethical theory (what the entry describes as the standard position).

it notes that peter singer treats the views synonymously.

as for me, i'm going to follow peter singer's approach and treat the terms synonymously.

i think that finer-grained distinctions, between theory and subject or between character and custom and so on, can be made in the situations where they are important, rather than trying to load them into the modern words ethics and morality.

in general, i think that debates about the "correct" meanings of closely related terms are often a fruitless distraction.
