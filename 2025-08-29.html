What are some of my core political commitments? All prescriptive political theories are fallible. We will never arrive at the final political theory which cannot be improved. An ideal form of society at any point along our trajectory is one in which multiple political ideologies coexist peacefully. Just as scientific pluralism does not mean that falsified theories can continue to flourish, so pluralism does not mean that failed ideologies like fascism should be tolerated.

One of the core traits of fascism is its anti-universalism. It is not trying to come up with a vision of the world that is ultimateley best for everyone. It's sort of like if we were all in a shared project of building a society, and one faction decides to kill everyone else so they can have everything to themselves. Fascism calls on particular groups of people to join it. It's saying, "Let's form a team, and together we'll dominate everyone else and control all the resources." As for the people who don't get on the team, fascism says that they can either die or become slaves.

Fascism is a threat of violence. If someone threatened to kill you, would you tolerate that just because "the rules say that everyone can say what they want"?

Gender

When I am unaware of the assumptions of my working theories, those assumptions appear to me as necessary truths. When gender is implicit, when it's just the water you swim in, it appears as an inviolable truth. When it becomes explicit, when you realize the ways you are actively performing gender, it becomes explicit and breakable.

Ideals

An ideal is a representation, not of how things are, but of how one would like things to be. It is nearly always a fiction, in that it describes a world which does not (yet) exist. So the first criterion for a useful ideal is that, even though it is not actual, it must be possible. And the burden of proof falls on the person promoting an ideal to demonstrate that it is possible.
